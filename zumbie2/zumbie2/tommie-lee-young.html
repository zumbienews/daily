Who is Tommie Lee Young?

Tommie Lee Young is a renowned American football coach and former player. He is best known for his successful coaching career at Tuskegee University, where he led the Golden Tigers to multiple national championships.

Born in 1938 in Notasulga, Alabama, Young began his football career as a standout player at Tuskegee Institute. After graduating, he embarked on a professional playing career in the Canadian Football League. However, it was in coaching that Young truly made his mark.

In 1974, Young became the head coach at Tuskegee University. Under his leadership, the Golden Tigers experienced unprecedented success. Young's teams won five national championships and produced numerous All-American players. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest coaches in college football history.

Beyond his coaching accomplishments, Young is also known for his advocacy for social justice and equality. He was a vocal supporter of the civil rights movement and has dedicated himself to improving opportunities for young people from underprivileged backgrounds.
