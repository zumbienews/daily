Who is Daniel Bergoli? Daniel Bergoli is an American rapper, songwriter, and social media personality best known for his song "Gucci Gang" which peaked at number three on the Billboard Hot 100.

Bergoli was born in Brooklyn, New York, on December 14, 1996. He began his music career in 2015, releasing his first mixtape, "2015," on SoundCloud. In 2016, he released his second mixtape, "1400 / 800," which featured the song "Gucci Gang." The song quickly went viral on social media and helped Bergoli gain a large following.

In 2017, Bergoli signed a record deal with Atlantic Records and released his debut album, "Lil Pump." The album peaked at number three on the Billboard 200 and featured the singles "Boss" and "D Rose." Bergoli has since released several more singles, including "I Love It" with Kanye West and "Welcome to the Party" with Offset and Quavo.

Bergoli is a controversial figure in the hip-hop community. He has been criticized for his lyrics, which some have called misogynistic and homophobic. However, he has also been praised for his unique style and his ability to connect with young people.
